Recall the puzzle of *Oracles and Chimeras* from the chapter on
Logical Thinking.

- *Ariadne* says: "Selene is an Oracle."
- *Selene* says: "All three of us are Oracles."
- *Thalia* says: "Ariadne is a Chimera."

Oracles always tell the truth; Chimeras always lie. Who is which?

## Translation to boolean algebra

Assign one yes/no variable to each figure: $a$ for Ariadne, $s$ for
Selene, $t$ for Thalia, with $a = \texttt{1}$ if Ariadne is an
Oracle and $a = \texttt{0}$ if she is a Chimera, and likewise for
$s$ and $t$.

Now we encode the data. The key observation is that an Oracle's
statement is true and a Chimera's statement is false, so a
speaker's type is itself the truth value of what the speaker says.

- *"Ariadne says that Selene is an Oracle"* tells us: if Ariadne is
  an Oracle then Selene is, and if Ariadne is a Chimera then Selene
  is. In one shot:

  $$
  a = s.
  $$

- *"Selene says that all three of us are Oracles"* tells us: if
  Selene is an Oracle then $ast = \texttt{1}$; if Selene is a
  Chimera then $ast = \texttt{0}$. In one shot:

  $$
  s = ast.
  $$

- *"Thalia says that Ariadne is a Chimera"* tells us: if Thalia is
  an Oracle then $a = \texttt{0}$; if Thalia is a Chimera then
  $a = \texttt{1}$. In one shot:

  $$
  t = \texttt{1} + a.
  $$

A valid type assignment is one for which all three equations hold
simultaneously.

## Solving the system

Pause to appreciate what has happened. The puzzle was handed to us
as three sentences in plain English; the three equations capture
exactly that information — nothing more, nothing less. Every drop
of meaning carried by the original wording has been poured into
these three equations, and from this point on we may forget the
wording entirely and let the algebra do the work.

Substitute $s = a$ from (i) into (ii):

$$
a = a \cdot a \cdot t = a^2 t = a t,
$$

using $a^2 = a$. So $a + at = \texttt{0}$, which factors as

$$
a(\texttt{1} + t) = \texttt{0}.
$$

Now use (iii), which says $t = \texttt{1} + a$, so
$\texttt{1} + t = \texttt{1} + \texttt{1} + a = a$. Substituting
back, the displayed equation becomes $a \cdot a = a^2 = \texttt{0}$,
and one more application of $a^2 = a$ gives $a = \texttt{0}$.
Equation (i) then gives $s = a = \texttt{0}$, and equation (iii)
gives $t = \texttt{1} + a = \texttt{1}$.

The unique solution is $a = \texttt{0}, s = \texttt{0}, t = \texttt{1}$:
**Ariadne and Selene are Chimeras, and Thalia is the lone Oracle.**
The puzzle has been solved by ordinary algebra — substitution,
factoring, and two uses of $a^2 = a$.

## What just happened

Look back at the steps. We did not draw a single case-table, and
we did not write the words "suppose Ariadne is an Oracle." We
translated each statement into an arithmetic equation, and from
there on the answer dropped out of substitution, factoring, and
the identity $a^2 = a$. There was no point at which we paused to
say "either this or that"; every branch of the original case
analysis has been swallowed into the algebra.

This is the payoff of the encoding. The case analysis we used in
the original chapter has been replaced by ordinary computation.
The arithmetic does the bookkeeping for us.

Five figures stand on the shore of the Isles of Paradox — Elara,
Orion, Phoebe, Selene, and Helios. Each is either an Oracle, who
always tells the truth, or a Chimera, who always lies. They speak
in turn:

- **Elara:** *"Orion is a Chimera."*
- **Orion:** *"Exactly two of us are Oracles."*
- **Phoebe:** *"Orion and Elara are either both Oracles or both Chimeras."*
- **Selene:** *"Phoebe and Elara are either both Oracles or both Chimeras."*
- **Helios:** *"Selene and Phoebe are either both Oracles or both Chimeras."*

Apply the same procedure as for the Treacherous Trio, noting that
each statement of the form "$X$ and $Y$ are either both Oracles or
both Chimeras" translates to the truth value $\texttt{1} + X + Y$
(zero when they disagree, one when they agree). Set up five
consistency equations in five yes/no variables, and use the algebra
to find every assignment that solves them all.

are told that all three messages are lies:

- **Middle chest:** *"All three chests have treasure."*
- **Right chest:** *"Only one of these chests has treasure."*

Assign yes/no variables $\ell, m, r$, where $\ell = \texttt{1}$
means the left chest holds treasure, and likewise for $m$ and $r$.
Translate each lying message, together with the background fact
that at least one chest holds treasure, into a boolean equation.
Solve the system to determine which chests are safe to open.
