Suppose we want a machine that accepts a binary string when at
least one of these holds:

- the count of `0`s is divisible by **3**, or
- the count of `1`s is divisible by **4**.

We already have machines for each property in isolation. The
question is how to put them together.

**The trick.** Run both machines *on the same tape, simultaneously*.
At every moment the combined state is the **pair** (state of A,
state of B). Reading a `0` advances A and leaves B alone; reading
a `1` does the reverse. The machine below does exactly that —
accept rule selectable.

<DualMachineRunner
  client:visible
  machineA={zerosMod3}
  machineB={onesMod4}
  defaultInput="000111"
  examples={["", "000", "1111", "000111", "0011", "000011110000"]}
  initialRule="OR"
  rules={["OR", "AND"]}
/>

The wiring is identical for OR and AND. The only difference is
which pairs we call accepting:

- **OR** — at least one component accepts. (Many pair-states
  qualify.)
- **AND** — both components accept. (Only the pair (A₀, B₀)
  qualifies.)

This is the **product construction**, and it's a remarkably
flexible tool. *Any* logical combination of two machines is just a
choice of accepting set on the same product wiring. We will use
this again on the next page to attack a sharper question.
