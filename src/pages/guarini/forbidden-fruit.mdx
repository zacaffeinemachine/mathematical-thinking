Four knights sit on a strange board: two blue knights on the top
corners of the central 3×3, two red knights on the bottom corners.
Between them, on the middle of each row, sits a **fruit**. The fruit
on the top row is blue; the fruit on the bottom row is red.

Knights are hungry creatures and not very discerning. The moment a
knight lands on a square containing a fruit, it eats the fruit —
without pausing to check its colour. This matters, because the two
fruits are not safe for every palate.

- A **red** knight can safely eat a **red** fruit.
- A **blue** knight can safely eat a **blue** fruit.
- But a red knight that lands on the **blue** fruit **dies on the
  spot**, and likewise a blue knight that lands on the red fruit
  dies.

Your task is the usual one: move the knights, one knight-jump at a
time, until the two blues occupy the bottom corners and the two reds
occupy the top corners. No two knights may share a square. The
squares marked with a cross cannot be landed on, though knights may
jump over them without trouble.

Keep every knight alive. If one of them eats a fruit of the wrong
colour, press **Reset** and start over. The fruits, of course, do
not move, and the two of them sit right in the middle of the
corridors you will most want to use.
