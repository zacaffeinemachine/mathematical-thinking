Before we move on to stranger boards, here is the puzzle the Italian
mathematician Paolo Guarini di Forlì set down in 1512 — the one that
started this whole line of thought.

It looks almost the same as the previous one: a 3×3 board, four
knights, swap the colours. The difference is small. The two red
knights now sit on the top two corners instead of on a diagonal; the
two blue knights sit on the bottom two.

Try it and see what changes.

If you have begun to believe that knight-swap puzzles are all much the
same, this one will test that belief gently. The move count, in
particular, is worth paying attention to — when it climbs higher than
you expected, ask yourself *why*.
