At last, here is the puzzle the Italian mathematician Paolo Guarini
di Forlì set down in 1512 — the one that started this whole line of
thought.

The starting position will look familiar from the previous page:
red knights across the top row, blue knights across the bottom. What
has changed is the goal. Guarini did not want the colours merely
interchanged row-for-row. He asked for the knights to be rearranged
into a very particular pattern: the two blue knights on one diagonal
of the board, and the two red knights on the other.

Take your time. Try short sequences and long ones. If you find
yourself retracing your steps, that is not a sign that you are
losing — quite the opposite. The patterns you notice, and the
patterns you *cannot* seem to find, are both telling you something.
