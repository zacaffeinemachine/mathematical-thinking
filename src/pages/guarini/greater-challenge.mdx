Here is a knight-swap puzzle on a stranger board: ten squares,
arranged in an irregular shape. Two red knights sit near the top; two
blue knights sit near the bottom. The task is the same as before —
swap the colours, one legal knight-jump at a time, with no two knights
ever on the same square.

Keep track of how many moves you need. Compare it with what the
earlier puzzles required. Notice, too, which squares act as
bottlenecks and which act as side-streets. Everything you observe here
is fuel for the chapter that follows.
