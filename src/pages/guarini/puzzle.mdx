In 1512, Paolo Guarini di Forlì posed the following problem. Place two
white knights in the top corners of a $3 \times 3$ board, and two black
knights in the bottom corners. Using only legal knight moves, swap them
— black on top, white on bottom.

It looks modest. Four pieces, nine squares. Try it.

<GuariniBoard client:visible />

If you played for a few minutes, you probably noticed something odd.
The knights seem to *orbit*. Each one keeps returning to the same set
of squares. The centre of the board never helps you. And no matter
what order you make the moves in, progress feels elusive.

This is the puzzle. The shift in perspective that solves it — almost
effortlessly — is the subject of the next page.
