Two puzzles for you to work through on your own. There is no
mind-over-matter button on this page: read each problem, try the board, and
think with a pencil.

## Exercise 1 — Reconfigure

Is it possible to rearrange the knights from the initial configuration to the
goal configuration shown below?

<ReconfigurePuzzle client:visible />

One can move only one knight at a time, and at no point should two different
knights occupy the same square. As you can see, this is not a standard
chessboard — a lot of the squares are missing. Knights cannot land on the
missing squares, but when jumping from one square to another, they can pretend
that the missing squares are present as intermediaries to help them move.

## Exercise 2 — Constrained Cavalry

There are four knights on a strange chessboard; two of the knights are blue
and the other two are red. There is an L-shaped **red land** (tinted pink)
and another L-shaped **blue land** (tinted blue).

<ConstrainedCavalryPuzzle client:visible />

**The task** is to transfer both red knights into the red land and both blue
knights into the blue land, under the following constraints:

- **No trespassing.** If a red knight so much as sets a hoof in the blue
  land, it dies (an extremely painful death). Similarly, if a blue knight
  enters the red land, it dies (an equally fun-to-watch death). The board
  lets you make the move — and then freezes. Press **Reset** to try again.
- **Be a gentle knight.** At any step, only one knight is allowed to move.
  You may, however, move the same knight many times in succession.
- **No cuddling.** At no point should two knights share a square.
- **Be within your limits.** The white squares — the ones marked only by the
  dashed grid — are forbidden to land on. Knights may, of course, hop over
  them.
