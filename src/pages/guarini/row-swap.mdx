Another 3×3 knight-swap puzzle, with a small change of starting
position. The red knights now sit side by side on the top row, and
the blue knights side by side on the bottom row. Your task is the
familiar one: rearrange the knights, one legal knight-jump at a time,
until the reds occupy the squares where the blues started, and the
blues occupy the squares where the reds did. Two knights may never
share a square.

Try it and see what changes.

If you have begun to believe that knight-swap puzzles are all much
the same, this one will test that belief gently. The move count, in
particular, is worth paying attention to — when it climbs higher
than you expected, ask yourself *why*.
