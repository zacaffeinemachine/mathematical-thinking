Here is the trick. Forget about the geometry of the chessboard. Draw a
dot for each square a knight might occupy — we can skip the centre,
since no knight move on a $3\times 3$ board ever lands there. Then
draw an edge between two dots whenever a knight can move between them
in one step.

Press **Reveal connections** below.

<GuariniGraph client:visible />

The eight squares that matter form a single loop — an $8$-cycle. The
knights' moves on the board are exactly steps along this loop.

Now watch the knights: on the board they jump all over the place, but
on the graph they simply *shift around the ring*. Press
**Shift ↻ clockwise** a few times and see.

Once you phrase the puzzle as "slide four tokens around a ring", the
solution is obvious. Two of the four tokens are white, two are black,
in the order $W, B, B, W$ around the cycle. You want $B, W, W, B$.
How many clockwise shifts does that take?

Try to get there. The next page will explain why this is not a trick —
it is the first hint of a general method.
