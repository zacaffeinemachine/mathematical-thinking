A page of puzzles for you to chew on. There is no *mind over matter*
button here: read each problem, work it out with paper and pen, and
sit with the silences when you get stuck.

## Exercise 1 — Five on the Shore

Welcome, brave logician, to the Isles of Paradox! On this mythical
shore, you have encountered five mysterious figures: **Elara**,
**Orion**, **Phoebe**, **Selene**, and **Helios**. Your task is to
pierce the veil of their words, for some are oracles who speak only
profound truths, while others are cunning chimeras who deal only in
deception.

**The crucial clue.** Chimeras always lie, and oracles always tell the
truth.

**Your task.** Given the following statements, identify who is an
oracle and who is a chimera.

- **Elara:** *"Orion is a chimera."*
- **Orion:** *"Exactly two of us are oracles."*
- **Phoebe:** *"Orion and Elara are either both oracles or both chimeras."*
- **Selene:** *"Phoebe and Elara are either both oracles or both chimeras."*
- **Helios:** *"Selene and Phoebe are either both oracles or both chimeras."*

## Exercise 2 — Four Hats in a Chest

In a chest there are four hats: two **red** and two **blue**. Three
wise sages &mdash; **Elara**, **Phoebe**, and **Orion** &mdash; are
gathered for a test of logic. Each sage is blindfolded and places one
of the four hats upon their head. They do not see the colour of the
hat they have chosen. One hat remains in the chest, its colour unknown
to them.

The blindfolds come off. The rules:

- Each sage can see the hats worn by the other two sages.
- No sage can see the hat on their own head.
- No sage can see the leftover hat in the chest.

The sages look at one another. Elara then makes the following silent
observation:

> *Phoebe is wearing a blue hat, and Orion is wearing a red hat.*

**The question.** Based on this information alone, how many of the
three sages know, with absolute certainty, the colour of their own hat?

## Exercise 3 — Six Hats

Three logicians &mdash; **Ariadne**, **Selene**, and **Thalia** &mdash;
are each wearing a hat. They can see the colours of the others' hats
but not their own. They know that their hats were drawn from a group
of five hats: **three red** and **two blue**. A question is posed to
each logician in order:

1. Ariadne is asked the colour of her hat. She responds, *"I don't know."*
2. Selene is asked the colour of her hat. She also says, *"I don't know."*
3. When Thalia is asked the colour of her hat, she answers correctly.

**The question.** What colour is Thalia's hat, and how did she know?

## Exercise 4 — Oracles and Chimeras

Before you stand three figures: **Ariadne**, **Selene**, and
**Thalia**. Each is either an **oracle** or a **chimera**. Oracles
always speak the truth; chimeras always lie. Each figure makes a
single statement:

- **Ariadne says:** *"Selene is an oracle."*
- **Selene says:** *"All three of us are oracles."*
- **Thalia says:** *"Ariadne is a chimera."*

**The question.** Who is an oracle, and who is a chimera?

## Exercise 5 — Treacherous Trio

Before you stand three figures: **Ariadne**, **Selene**, and
**Thalia**. Each is either an **oracle** (always tells the truth) or a
**chimera** (always lies). Each figure makes a single statement:

- **Ariadne says:** *"There is exactly one chimera amongst the three
  of us, and it is not me."*
- **Selene says:** *"Ariadne is an oracle."*
- **Thalia says:** *"Ariadne and Selene are both oracles."*

**The question.** Who is an oracle, and who is a chimera?

## Exercise 6 — Olympian Cards

High atop the peaks of Mount Olympus, a game of pure logic unfolds.
**Zeus**, the King of Gods, has fashioned a deck of six celestial
cards, each inscribed with a distinct integer from the set
$\{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}$. He summons **Athena**, the Goddess of Wisdom,
and **Apollo**, the God of Truth. He deals one secret card to Athena
and one secret card to Apollo. Each deity sees only their own card.
Zeus then proclaims to them both:

> *"The numbers you hold sum up to either **5** or **7**."*

A silence falls, followed by this exchange.

- **Athena speaks:** *"Father, despite my wisdom, I do not know
  whether our sum is 5 or 7."*
- **Apollo speaks:** *"Hearing this, sister, I too must confess: I do
  not know whether our sum is 5 or 7."*

**Hermes**, the Messenger God, has witnessed this entire exchange from
the shadows. Being a perfect logician himself, he begins to deduce the
truth.

**The questions.**

1. Which specific card numbers can Hermes be absolutely certain
   **Athena does not possess**?
2. Which specific card numbers can Hermes be absolutely certain
   **Apollo does not possess**?

## Exercise 7 — Counting Chimeras (a)

There are **10 beings** lined up in a row, each one either a chimera
or an oracle.

- The first being says, *"Exactly **one** of us 10 is a chimera."*
- The second being says, *"Exactly **two** of us 10 are chimeras."*
- The third being says, *"Exactly **three** of us 10 are chimeras."*
- $\vdots$
- The tenth being says, *"Exactly **ten** of us 10 are chimeras."*

**The question.** How many chimeras are there?

## Exercise 8 — Counting Chimeras (b)

There are **10 beings** lined up in a row, each one either a chimera
or an oracle.

- The first being says, *"At least **one** of us 10 is a chimera."*
- The second being says, *"At least **two** of us 10 are chimeras."*
- The third being says, *"At least **three** of us 10 are chimeras."*
- $\vdots$
- The tenth being says, *"At least **ten** of us 10 are chimeras."*

**The question.** How many chimeras are there?

## Exercise 9 — Counting Chimeras (c)

There are **infinitely many** beings lined up in a queue, each one
either a chimera or an oracle.

- The first being says, *"The being behind me is an oracle."*
- The second being says, *"The being behind me is a chimera."*
- The third being says, *"The being behind me is an oracle."*
- The fourth being says, *"The being behind me is a chimera."*
- $\vdots$

It is known that the first being is an oracle. Choose the correct
options.

a. The 42nd being is an oracle.
b. The 43rd being is an oracle.
c. The 44th being is an oracle.
d. The 45th being is an oracle.

## Exercise 10 — Athena, Apollo, and the Off-by-One Cards

High atop the peaks of Mount Olympus, a second game of pure logic
unfolds. **Zeus** has fashioned a deck of *infinitely* many celestial
cards, each inscribed with a distinct integer from the set
$\{1, 2, 3, \ldots\}$. He summons **Athena** and **Apollo**, shows
them this celestial deck, and then deals one secret card to each.
Each deity sees only their own card. Zeus then proclaims:

> *"The numbers you two hold differ by 1."*

A silence falls, followed by this exchange.

- **Apollo speaks:** *"I do not know what card Athena holds."*
- **Athena speaks:** *"Brother, I also do not know what your card
  number is."*
- **Apollo speaks:** *"Ah, now I know your card number! And my own
  card bears an even number."*

**The questions.**

1. Which card number does Apollo hold?
2. Which card number does Athena hold?
