Three perfect logicians &mdash; **Elara**, **Phoebe**, and **Orion** &mdash;
were given a puzzle. A hat, coloured either red or blue, was placed on
each of their heads. They could each see the hats of the other two, but
not their own.

After a moment of looking at one another, they all spoke at the same
time:

> *"I see at least one red hat!"*

A silence then fell as they thought about this statement. Then, all at
once, they spoke again:

> *"I don't know what colour my hat is."*

After a little more time passed, a realisation struck all three.
Together, they exclaimed:

> *"Now I know my hat colour!"*

**The question.** What is the colour of each person's hat? And, more
importantly, what is the chain of reasoning that led the three of them
from uncertainty to certainty?

The credit lies entirely in the reasoning behind the answer, not in
the answer itself. Sit with the three rounds before peeking. Each
sentence the logicians utter &mdash; including the silences in
between &mdash; carries information.
