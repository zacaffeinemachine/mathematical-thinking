Four perfectly rational logicians (named **1**, **2**, **3**, and **4**)
have been captured by a warden. To win their freedom, they must solve a
puzzle. The warden places them in a line as shown in the diagram.

- **Logician 1** is at the highest position and can see the hats of
  Logician 2 and Logician 3.
- **Logician 2** is in the middle and can see the hat of Logician 3.
- **Logician 3** is in front of Logician 2 and can see only a brick
  wall.
- **Logician 4** is on the other side of the brick wall, unable to see
  anyone, and no one can see them.

The warden places a hat on each logician's head from a set of **two red
hats** and **two blue hats**. No logician can see the colour of their
own hat.

Here is the important fine print.

1. **Perfect logic.** All four are perfect logicians. They will make
   any deduction that is logically possible, and each knows that the
   other three are equally perfect.
2. **Shared knowledge.** Every logician knows the entire setup &mdash;
   the positions, the lines of sight, and that there are exactly two
   red and two blue hats in total.
3. **No communication.** No speaking, no gestures &mdash; logicians
   answer the warden's questions and nothing more.
4. **Certainty required.** A logician will state their hat colour only
   when they are 100% certain. They will never guess.

The warden then conducts the following round of questioning, and every
logician hears every question and every answer.

1. The warden asks **Logician 4**: *Do you know the colour of your
   hat?* &nbsp; Logician 4 replies, *"I do not know."*
2. The warden asks **Logician 3**: *Do you know the colour of your
   hat?* &nbsp; Logician 3 replies, *"I do not know."*
3. The warden asks **Logician 2**: *Do you know the colour of your
   hat?* &nbsp; Logician 2 replies, *"I do not know."*
4. The warden asks **Logician 1** &mdash; the one with the best view
   &mdash; *Do you know the colour of your hat?* &nbsp; Logician 1 also
   replies, *"I do not know."*

And then, out of the silence, **Logician 2** suddenly speaks up and
correctly announces their hat colour.

**The question.** What is the step-by-step reasoning that lets
Logician 2 be absolutely certain of their hat colour?

Sit with this for a few minutes before peeking. The whole puzzle
turns on what one of the four "I don't know"s really means.
